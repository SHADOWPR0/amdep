# Product Thesis

Field-heavy companies leak margin through poor deployment of people, supervisors, equipment, and emerging robotic assets.

The visible problem looks like scheduling. The real problem is operational allocation under constraints. A job is not ready just because a calendar slot is open. A job needs the right skills, the right certifications, the right equipment, a supervisor who can actually manage the work, a crew that can operate together, a route that does not punish the worker, and enough field proof to get paid later.

AmDep turns field deployment from tribal knowledge into measurable decision intelligence.

## The Leak

Bad deployment compounds:

- a foreman drives past a better job
- a closer qualified worker is ignored because the dispatcher remembers a familiar crew
- a supervisor manages jobs scattered across the map
- a scarce certification blocks several jobs
- a strong worker receives repeated long-haul assignments and burns out
- equipment sits idle while the wrong truck moves
- inspection work consumes supervisor time that a drone or capture unit could handle

Every one of those misses is explainable. Few are measured.

## The Product Bet

The first product should not replace the customer’s ERP, payroll, CRM, or field app. The first product should sit above existing exports and answer one hard question:

**Where is deployment waste hiding this week?**

From there, the product expands into live dispatch recommendations, field evidence capture, robotics deployment, and enterprise APIs.

## The Fastest Wedge

Start with deployment audits for roofing, restoration, storm response, utility contractors, and fiber/telecom field operators. These buyers feel urgency, run scattered work, move crews and equipment constantly, and understand route burn without needing a software sermon.

The opener is simple:

Give AmDep the last 30 days of assignments, crews, jobsites, skills, certifications, and supervisor mappings. AmDep returns a waste map, dispatch delta, burden-risk table, and savings estimate.

## Product Standard

AmDep should earn trust by showing the work:

- every recommendation has a reason
- every cost has an assumption
- every risk score is labeled
- every hard constraint is visible
- every customer claim waits for customer proof

The attitude can be sharp. The math has to be sober.
